Instructions:

This website is used to play Checkers, a board game for two players. The goal is to remove your opponent's pieces until they don't have any pieces on the board. To move a piece you have to move away from your side of the board by one space diagonally. If there is an enemy piece in the way but an empty space 2 spaces away you can jump over the enemy's piece and remove it from the board. If you successfully jump and there is another jump available for the current piece being moved you can keep jumping and removing pieces. Once you reach the end of the board your piece becomes a king. Kings can move in any direction but still move the same distance (one square at a time) or by jumping in any direction.

To play you can either click or drag a piece. If you click a checker you can then click a gray square to move there. Or you can drag a checker to a gray square and then drop it onto that square. The square is gray to show that square is a valid move. Jumps are forced so if there is at least 1 jump available you can choose among those available but cannot move any other piece. The piece forced to jump will have a black square around the checker piece to show you.

Advanced Instructions:

If you want to play with at least one computer player then you must mark either one or both of the checkboxes for whichever computer you want to use. Make sure to wait for the computer to finish their move, the label "Computer is idle." will swap to "Computer is thinking." when the computer is active. If you want to start the game with the computer as black then you have to press the "Start/Continue AI move" button once. Then the computer will automatically make a move when it's their turn.
